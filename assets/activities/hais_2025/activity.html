I had the opportunity to present our work “Perplexity, Uncertainty, and the Limits of Active Learning” at the 20th International Conference on Hybrid Artificial Intelligence Systems (HAIS 2025), held in Salamanca, Spain. The presentation sparked insightful discussions around the challenges of Active Learning with Large Language Models and how dataset complexity—particularly in terms of perplexity and uncertainty—influences performance and generalization.

Sharing our findings with the research community allowed me to exchange ideas with experts in NLP and hybrid AI systems, gaining valuable feedback on potential directions for improving Active Learning robustness. The session emphasized the growing interest in data-efficient LLM training and reinforced the importance of exploring new hybrid and uncertainty-aware query strategies.

Participating in HAIS 2025 was a rewarding experience that not only highlighted the relevance of our research within the AI community but also strengthened collaborations for future work on adaptable and trustworthy learning systems.
